Welcome to my homepage!


Born and brought up in Dharmavaram, famous for its "Silk sarees". Received my B.S. in ICT from DA-IICT in India, M.S. in Computer Engineering and Ph.D. in Cloud Computing from the University of Florida. I worked as a Research Assistant with Prof. Janise McNair and was a part of Wireless and Mobile Laboratory, and Small Satellite Design Club at University of Florida. I co-founded Edusoftware under the guidance of Prof. Prabhat Ranjan. My interests include Cloud Computing, Big Data, Small Satellites, Open Source, Distributed Systems and Crypto Currencies.

Hobbies and Interests


Free Software and Open Access: A free culture is one where all members are free to participate in the transmission and evolution of our culture. Open access is the practice of providing unrestricted access via the Internet to peer-reviewed scholarly research. I believe that free software and open access will be the foundation to humanity in the age of information and technology. They are essential for protecting humanity from becoming a slave to few wealthy people and organizations.


GNU, Linux, Ubuntu and Android: GNU/Linux is a computer operating system developed in the spirit of free and open source software. Development of GNU was initiated by Richard Stallman in 1983 and Linus Torvalds spearheads the development of the Linux, the biggest colloborative project in the history of mankind. Ubuntu and Android are GNU/Linux distributions for Desktop and Mobile ecosystems. These projects embody the spirit of open and free software and help protect the freedoms of humanity.


Python: Python is a general-purpose programming language whose design philosophy emphasizes human friendliness. Python is easy to learn, has powerful built-in libraries and has very elegant syntax which makes programming fun.


CubeSat: CubeSat is a very small satellite, about the size of baseball. It has potential to democratize the access to space and make space research accessable to everyone. It can potentially save millions of lives and livelihoods, especially in third world countries, through better weather monitoring and forecasting, flood and draught prediction, volcanic and glacial activity monitoring. For more information please refer to my research "CubeSat Cloud: A Framework for Distributed Storage, Processing and Communication of Remote Sensing Data on CubeSat Clusters"


Bitcoin: Bitcoin is an open source peer-to-peer currency introduced by "Satoshi Nakamoto". Bitcoin and virtual currencies have potential to revolutinize todays banking system and provide free and easy banking services to anyone who owns a mobile phone. Bitcoin uses cryptography for security and is not controlled by any single authority or government.


Activities


GatorLUG: The primary purpose of this organization is to help create and develop awareness about Open Source Software and help students learn various Open Source Software like Python, Perl, Linux, Ubuntu, Android, etc ... Check out our classes at GatorLUG docs.


ICEC: Indian Cultural and Eduction Centre: Gainesville's local hub of Indian students and Indian families living in Gainesville. We host cultural events through out the year ranging from Diwali, Eid, Christmas and the Annual India Fest showcasing and selling wide variety of Indian Food.


SSDC: Small Satellite Design Club: The UF Small Satellite Design Club (SSDC) is a student led organization dedicated to the design, fabrication, testing, and flight of small satellite technology. Currently we are working hard towards our first Cubesat mission nicknamed "SwampSat". The SwampSat mission shall demonstrate rapid retargeting and precision pointing (R2P2) maneuvers using miniaturized control moment gyroscopes (CMGs) developed at the University of Florida (UF).


Outdoor Adventure Recreation: Outdoor Adventure Recreation provides the UF community with an organization where people of all skill levels can enjoy outdoor activites. Emphasis is placed on members learning to be competent at their activities of interest and not merely being led. There is no requirement of experience, dues, or special equipment. OAR trains its members, runs trips at cost, and provides necessary equipment.
